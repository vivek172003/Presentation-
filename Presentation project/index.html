Introduction to Retail

What is Retail?

A business model focused on selling goods and services directly to end consumers, with at least 80% of sales to end-users. Includes:

  • Physical stores
  • Vending machines
  • Door-to-door sales
  • Online platforms

Industry Significance

  • 5th largest industry in India
  • 2nd largest employer after agriculture with 45–50 million people
  • Evolving from traditional to modern formats

History of Credit Cards (1920s-1950s)

Charge Plates (1920s)

Department stores and gas stations pioneered credit with metal plates

  • Allowed store-specific credit purchases
  • Metal plates/coins for customer identification
  • Limited to single establishments
→

Charga-Plates (1930s-1940s)

Evolution of store credit systems

  • Embossed metal cards
  • Retailer-specific usage
  • Improved record keeping
→

"Charg-It" Card (1946)

First bank-issued credit system by John Biggins

  • Closed-loop payment system
  • Bank-managed billing
  • Multi-merchant acceptance
→

Diners Club Card (1950)

First general-purpose credit card by Frank McNamara

  • Multi-location acceptance
  • Focus on travel & entertainment
  • Pioneered modern credit system

Key Factors in Credit Card Rise

Convenience

Eliminated need for cash carrying

Encouraged consumer spending

Retail Industry Sectors

Retail Store Strategy Mix

Integrated combination of hours, location, service, advertising, and prices that defines a retailer's approach

1. Convenience Stores

Small neighborhood stores offering essential items with extended hours for customer convenience.

Why Here: Prioritize accessibility & extended hours, perfect for immediate needs.

Ex: 7-Eleven, Circle K, 24SEVEN, EasyDay

2. Grocery Stores

Traditional food retailers specializing in fresh produce, packaged foods, and household essentials.

Why Here: Focus on fresh produce & household essentials, catering to daily needs.

Ex: Kroger, Safeway, Reliance Fresh, Spencer's

3. Supermarkets

Mid-sized food stores offering a wide assortment of groceries and household items in a self-service format.

Why Here: Balance variety, pricing, and convenience for weekly or monthly shopping.

Ex: Trader Joe's,Whole Foods, Big Bazaar, More

4. Hypermarkets

Large-scale food retailers combining groceries with general merchandise.

Why Here: Offer a one-stop shopping experience, combining groceries with general merchandise.

Ex: Walmart, Tesco, Reliance Smart Bazaar, Star Bazaar

5. Q-Commerce (Quick Commerce)

Ultra-fast delivery services promising groceries, snacks, and daily essentials within 10–30 minutes.

Why Here: Focus on speed, convenience, and impulse buying, typically app-based and location-optimized.

Ex: Gopuff, Jokr, Zepto, Blinkit, Swiggy Instamart

6. Specialty Retailers

Focused stores offering deep assortments in specific categories with expert knowledge.

Why Here: Provide specialized products & expert knowledge, enhancing customer experience.

Ex: Best Buy, PetSmart, Croma, FirstCry, Lenskart

7. Home Improvement Stores

Retailers selling tools, hardware, paint, plumbing, and materials for home renovations.

Why Here: Provide all-in-one solutions for DIY and professional home renovation needs.

Ex: Home Depot, Lowe’s, Asian Paints, Jaquar World, CERA

8. Drug / pharmacy Stores

Pharmacies providing medications, health products, and personal care items.

Why Here: Essential for health & wellness, offering medications and consultations.

Ex: CVS, Walgreens, Apollo Pharmacy, MedPlus, Netmeds

9. Department Stores

Large-format retailers offering diverse categories under one roof.

Why Here: Provide a wide range of products, from clothing to home goods, in one location.

Ex: Macy’s, JCPenney, Shoppers Stop, Central, Lifestyle

10. Big Box Stores

Massive retail outlets selling a wide range of products, often located in standalone buildings or power centers.

Why Here: Offer deep discounts through scale and category-wide inventory under one roof.

Ex: Costco, Best Buy, Metro Wholesale, Reliance Market

11. Discount Stores

Value-oriented retailers offering reduced prices through efficient operations.

Why Here: Focus on cost efficiency & low prices, appealing to budget-conscious consumers.

Ex: Dollar General, Aldi, DMart, Vishal Mega Mart

12. Omnichannel Stores

Retailers operating across physical stores, websites, apps, and catalogs.

Why Here: Provide a seamless shopping experience across multiple channels, enhancing convenience.

Ex: Walmart, Target, Reliance Retail, Tata CLiQ, Shoppers Stop

13. Direct Sales & Catalogs

Traditional direct-to-consumer retailers using catalogs and personal selling.

Why Here: Focus on personalized service & catalog shopping, appealing to niche markets.

Ex: Avon, Mary Kay, Amway India, Tupperware India, Oriflame India

14. E-commerce

Online-only retailers operating exclusively through digital platforms.

Why Here: Provide 24/7 shopping convenience, appealing to tech-savvy consumers.

Ex: Amazon, Alibaba, Flipkart, Meesho, AJIO, Nykaa
